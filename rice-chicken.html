Chicken and Rice Recipe

Description:

Here's a simple and delicious recipe for chicken and rice that is easy to prepare and comforting to enjoy.

This one-pot meal is perfect for a weeknight dinner and can be adapted with various ingredients to suit your taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450 g) chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on for more flavor, or boneless, skinless for a leaner option)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups chicken broth (you can adjust the amount depending on the rice type)
  • teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or any herbs of your choice like rosemary or oregano)
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)
  • Lemon wedges and fresh parsley for garnish

Cooking Steps:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    • Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.

      This removes excess starch and helps prevent the rice from being too sticky.

    • Dice the onion and bell pepper, and mince the garlic.
    • Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces and season with salt and pepper.

  2. Brown the Chicken:

    • Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium-high heat.
    • Add the chicken pieces to the pan and cook until they are golden brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.

      Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.

  3. Saut√© the Vegetables:

    • In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed, then add the onion and bell pepper.
    • Cook for about 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are soft.
    • Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  4. Cook the Rice:

    • Add the rice to the pan with the vegetables and stir to combine.
    • Cook for about 2 minutes, stirring frequently, to lightly toast the rice.
    • Add the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a boil.
    • Return the browned chicken to the pan.

      Add any additional herbs or spices (like a bay leaf or dried thyme).

  5. Simmer:

    • Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan.

      Let the mixture simmer for about 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.

  6. Finish and Serve:

    • Remove the pan from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes.

      This allows the rice to finish absorbing any residual liquid without overcooking.

    • Fluff the rice with a fork, then taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
    • Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro if desired.
